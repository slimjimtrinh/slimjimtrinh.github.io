Preparing for an Interview

You are usually called in for an interview when an employer see's potential in you after looking at your resume. Now is your time shine and prove them that you are the one made for the job. First impressions are super important, it takes about 7 seconds for them to first judge you on your appearance, attitude, and professionalism.

Appearance

Appearance is important because thats probably what the employer first see's. You need to be dressed appropriately for the job you are applying for. Don't over dress if your job position does not require it on a basis. If your job was for a foods place, you would want to appear more tidy and clean. Always make sure you don't have have strong stench of body odor but also not over coloned or perfumed.

Body Language

You body language tells a lot about you. As you meet the employer, you want to show up with a smile and greeting the employer with a polite greeting and firm handshake. As you talk to them, make sure you seem attentive as they take and be a good listener by looking interested. Your posture as you sit makes a difference too, make you you are sitting straight but yet relaxed to not look stiff. Avoid shaking or bouncing your leg as you sit and figiting with your hand. If you have trouble staying still, try holding a pen in your hand but don't make it a distraction.

Materials you should bring

If you appear with a notebook and pen, it make you seem more interested as you are able to take notes. Take notes on what is important to you but do not just write everything down word for word.
